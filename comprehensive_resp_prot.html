Respiratory Protection Module


Comprehensive Respiratory Protection Program

Respiratory protection is a vital component of TB IC programs in healthcare facilities. In the hierarchy of TB IC measures, respiratory protection represents the “last line of defense” in protecting HCWs from TB exposures in instances where exposures cannot adequately, or predictably, be prevented by using established administrative and environmental controls. When used properly, respirators further reduce healthcare workers’ risk of inhaling infectious airborne particles. However, provision of respirators to staff is only one component of a respiratory protection program.

This module outlines the components of, and a step-wise approach to, implementing a comprehensive respiratory protection program within health facilities, including development of written policies and procedures; assignment of responsibility; selection of respirators; medical evaluations; fit testing; staff education and training; respirator reuse, maintenance, and storage; and recordkeeping. These steps are necessary to ensuring proper respirator use and enhancing worker and workplace safety.
