Respiratory Protection Module


Written Policies and Procedures for Respiratory Prtoection Program

Developing written policies and procedures are a critical initial step in establishing a Respiratory Protection Program (RPP). These policies will govern the overall procedures for the RPP and address indications for respirator use (situations and staff) as well as provide detailed operating procedures for routine use by HCWs. The RPP policy should include all staff working in the facility, even if they are not direct employees of the healthcare facility (e.g., contactors, trainees, staff from other agencies). Sample templates, that can be adapted for local use, are available to help guide development of policies and procedures for RPPs in healthcare facilities.


Evaluation of the Respiratory Protection Program

There should be a documented procedure for periodic evaluation of the RPP to help identify and correct programmatic deficiencies; the evaluation should include obtaining feedback from HCWs who use respirators. For access to a program checklist, please click here.


Documentation and Recordkeeping

Written copies of the facility’s policies, procedures, and whom to contact for questions or additional information, as well as local or national RPP standards should be available to all staff, and kept in the program administrator’s office. Copies of evaluations and revisions also should be documented and made available upon request. Confidential records of HCWs’ fit tests and medical evaluations, should be securely maintained by the RPA, as well as the examining health care provider.
